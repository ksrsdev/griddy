Interaction Chain:
	Run play:
		>Trench Battle
		>Point of Attack
		>Open Field
	Pass play:
		>Trench Battle = pressure clock
		>WR separation (depends on check WR and CB vs throw window (dictated by pressure))
		>Resolution: If QB has enough time he can make the throw otherwise he has to decide: Sack, scramble, throw away

4. A Refined "Universal" Chain

To keep your code simple, use this 3-Step Universal Check for every play:

    The Advantage Roll: Offense_OVR vs Defense_OVR + Formation_Modifier (e.g., Pink gets a bonus to passing, Brown gets a bonus to rushing).

    The Interaction:

        If Run: Trench Battle -> RB vs LB.

        If Pass: Trench Battle (Pressure) -> WR vs DB.

    The Result: Calculate total yards and update the Game State (Ball Position, Down, Clock).

1. The MVP Logic Flow (The "Chunk" Method)

Instead of 22 individual player rolls, break the play into 3 Static Checks. Each check passes a "Bonus" or "Penalty" to the next one.
Check 1: The Trench Battle (OL vs. DL)

    Formula: (Offense_OL_Group_OVR + Formation_Bonus) - (Defense_DL_Group_OVR)

    The Roll: Random(1-20) + Formula

    Result: This generates your "Line Push" (Cumulative Success starts here).

        Example: Brown (T-Form) gets a +10 Run Bonus. Pink (3-Man Line) gets a -10 Penalty. Brown wins the trenches easily. Success = +20.

Check 2: The Point of Attack (Lead Blockers vs. LBs)

    Formula: (FB_OVR + RB_OVR) - (LB_Group_OVR)

    The Roll: Random(1-20) + Formula + Line_Push

    Result: This determines if the RB gets past the line of scrimmage.

        Example: Brown’s 2 FBs and RB are huge. Pink’s 2 LBs are dropped in zone (Penalty to run defense). Brown smashes them. Success = +45.

Check 3: The Open Field (RB vs. DBs/Safeties)

    Formula: (RB_OVR) - (DB_Group_OVR)

    The Roll: Random(1-20) + Formula + Success_From_Check_2

    Final Result: This determines the Yardage.

        Success < 0: Tackle for Loss.

        Success 0–20: 2–5 yard gain.

        Success 21–50: 10–20 yard gain.

        Success 51+: TOUCHDOWN.

2. Why the "Formation Modifier" is your Secret Weapon

In your Brown vs. Pink example, the Formation Modifier handles the "Common Sense" part of football:

    Brown (T-Formation): Gives a +15 bonus to "Check 1" and "Check 2" for Runs.

    Pink (3-2-6 Zone): Gives a +15 bonus to "Check 3" for Passes, but a -15 penalty to "Check 1" for Runs.

Because Pink is in a "Soft Zone," your Check 3 roll for Brown will be much harder. Even though Brown's RB "won" the first two checks, Pink has 6 guys (DBs/Safeties) waiting. In the code, this means Pink gets a Defensive Bonus on the final "Open Field" check, making a TD unlikely even if the run is "successful."
3. MVP Implementation (Step-by-Step)

    Group the Stats: Don't calculate 11 people. Average them into OL, DL, LB, DB, WR, QB, RB. (Except for the Star, use their 100 OVR specifically in their group).

    Assign the Modifiers: Create a simple table where T-Formation + Run = +15 and Empty + Pass = +15.

    The "Text Result" Builder: Use your "Success" number to pick the text.

        Success > 40: "He's into the secondary! One man to beat!"

        Success < 0: "Swarmed in the backfield! Nowhere to go."

4. Animation vs. Text

Stick to text for now. If you can make the text report feel exciting (e.g., "Brown's massive line just deleted the Pink front three!"), you’ve won. Animation is just "Visualizing the Math"—if the math isn't fun, the animation will just show a boring play in 2D.
